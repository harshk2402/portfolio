Derivatives Pricing Engine in C++: Lattice, Black–Scholes & Monte Carlo

C++
Numerical Methods
Monte Carlo
Repository

This project brings together a range of core techniques for pricing derivatives, implemented in modern C++. It combines three complementary approaches: lattice methods (binomial and trinomial) for pricing European and American vanilla options, as well as exotic structures such as digital and spread options; closed‑form Black–Scholes formulas for analytical benchmarking; and Monte Carlo simulation for path‑dependent products like Asian options, including control variates and bump‑and‑reprice Greeks. The engine separates models, payoffs, and numerical components, making it straightforward to extend to other contracts. Developed iteratively through coursework, it now provides a clear and performant base for both experimentation and professional use.

Key Features

  • Lattice pricing (CRR binomial / trinomial) with European, American, digital & spread payoffs
  • Closed‑form Black–Scholes for benchmarking against lattice outputs
  • Monte Carlo for path‑dependent options (Asian) with control‑variate and Greeks
